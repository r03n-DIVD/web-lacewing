## What this phase is about

Project Lacewing does not begin with scanning — it begins with building the foundation that makes responsible, independent, AI-driven security research possible at scale. Before we deploy a single model, we need the right people, the right partners, and the right plan.

Project Initiation is the phase in which we establish all of that.

## What we are doing

### Building the partner ecosystem

Project Lacewing depends on a diverse coalition: organisations that contribute funding, hardware, volunteer expertise, or access to their own codebases for research. During this phase we are actively identifying, approaching, and onboarding founding partners — and defining what a sustainable, long-term partnership model looks like.

### Defining the research roadmap

The subsequent sub-projects — Open Source Models, Closed Source Models, Investigation of Software, Data Leak Discovery, and Vulnerable Configuration Research — each need a clear scope, timeline, and success criteria. This phase produces that roadmap, informed by input from the security community, our partners, and DIVD's own research experience.

### Setting up the infrastructure

Running AI-assisted vulnerability research requires infrastructure: compute, tooling, secure data handling, and integration with DIVD's existing case management and responsible disclosure workflows. During initiation we design and begin building that foundation.

### Establishing governance

Independence is only meaningful if it is structurally protected. This phase establishes the governance model that ensures Project Lacewing remains non-commercial, transparent, and aligned with DIVD's code of conduct — regardless of which partners join or how the project grows.
