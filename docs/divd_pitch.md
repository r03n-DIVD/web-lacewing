# The internet belongs to everyone. So does its defense.

## What's happening

On April 7, 2026, Anthropic launched **Project Glasswing** — a large-scale initiative using AI to find and fix vulnerabilities in critical software. Sounds great. And it is, partly.

But look who's at the table: Amazon, Apple, Microsoft, Google, NVIDIA, JPMorgan, Cisco — and the Linux Foundation. The latter is a welcome exception, but doesn't change the fundamental dynamic: it runs on a closed, non-public AI model (Claude Mythos Preview) that you can only access if you're among the chosen few. After the preview period, access costs $25–$125 per million tokens. The decisions about what gets found, what gets fixed, and who benefits are largely in the hands of a small group of American tech companies and investors.

This isn't a criticism of Glasswing. But it is a signal: **if Europe leaves this to others, Europe loses its voice in the security of its own digital infrastructure.**

---

## What DIVD can do

DIVD has something no Glasswing partner has: **a track record of independent, non-profit, responsible disclosure — with no agenda other than making the internet safer.**

We propose to launch **Project Lacewing¹** — a European, open, community-driven answer. No closed club. No proprietary models we depend on. Instead, targeted use of AI to find and fix vulnerabilities in critical open source software and at companies that are willing to engage.

Concretely:
- We raise funds to invest in **AI tokens and locally running open-source models** (and the hardware to run them on).
- We deploy that capacity for **automated vulnerability research** — at the scale DIVD can handle, but smarter and faster.
- Companies and organizations can contribute with **money, hardware, people, or codebase access** — and in return commit to fixing what we find.
- Everything we find and learn, **we share — but responsibly.** As always, through responsible disclosure: the affected party first, then the world.

---

## Why now

The barrier to finding vulnerabilities is structurally dropping. What Glasswing demonstrates is that AI is already better than most humans at finding bugs that have sat undetected in critical software for ten, twenty, sometimes thirty years. That's not going to reverse.

The question isn't whether these capabilities are coming. The question is **who has them.**

If we do nothing, the answer in two years will be: a handful of American companies, a few Asian state actors, and the criminals catching up with them. DIVD can be a third way — transparent, independent, European.

---

## What we need

This project cannot succeed without substantial funding — on a scale DIVD has not been able to reach before, because running and training AI models is not cheap. But the alternative is watching others set the standard.

We're looking for people within DIVD who want to:
- Help think through the **technical setup** (which models, which hardware, which targets)
- Help with **fundraising** (grants, partners, donors)
- Help **lead and champion** this effort

The internet belongs to everyone. Let's make sure its defense does too.

---

## Note

¹ Project Lacewing is named after the *lacewing* (family Chrysopidae) — an insect with transparent, lace-like wings, much like the glasswing butterfly after which Anthropic's initiative is named. But there is a meaningful difference: the lacewing is a natural predator of pests. With AI, we can surface so many vulnerabilities that they become a plague — and the Lacewing is here to fight it.
